Decatur is the home of North Alabama's white BBQ sauce, a 1920s Big Bob Gibson invention that turned the BBQ-sauce playbook upside down with a mayo-and-vinegar base. The sauce remains North-Alabama-locked despite occasional national food-media coverage.

This list is almost certainly incomplete; Decatur and the Huntsville-Decatur corridor hold further hyper-local dishes that have not yet surfaced in the survey.

> **A note on the Where-to-eat blocks.** Every entry below carries a list of restaurants and, where available, star ratings as of the date this post was published. These are a snapshot. Verify hours and addresses before driving anywhere.

### White BBQ Sauce — Decatur, AL (North Alabama)

A mayo-vinegar-horseradish-pepper sauce created in the 1920s by Big Bob Gibson, a railroad worker turned pitmaster in Decatur. He dunked entire smoked chickens into vats of it. Still primarily a North Alabama tradition concentrated in the Decatur/Huntsville corridor. Big Bob Gibson Bar-B-Q remains the canonical source (90+ years in operation). The sauce has gained some food-media recognition but remains functionally absent from BBQ restaurants outside Alabama. The national BBQ conversation fixates on KC tomato-based, Texas post oak, NC vinegar, or SC mustard sauces, and most Americans have never heard of a mayo-based BBQ sauce.

*Sources: Southern Kissed (2026); HH Lifestyle Travel (2023, firsthand); Journeyscape (2025). Cross-confirmed.*

**Where to eat:** [Big Bob Gibson Bar-B-Q](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Big+Bob+Gibson+Bar-B-Q+Decatur+AL), 1715 6th Ave SE, Decatur (the canonical 90+ year original). Multiple Huntsville-Decatur barbecue joints carry the sauce.
