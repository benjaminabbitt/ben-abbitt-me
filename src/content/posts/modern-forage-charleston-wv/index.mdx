Charleston is the corporate home of Tudor's Biscuit World, the from-scratch biscuit-sandwich chain that has stayed inside West Virginia's borders since its 1980 founding. The named-customer biscuit sandwiches (the Mary B, the Ron, the Peppi) are local cultural shorthand.

This list is almost certainly incomplete; Charleston and the Kanawha Valley hold further hyper-local dishes that have not yet surfaced in the survey.

### Tudor's Biscuit World — West Virginia (statewide)

A WV-only fast food chain (founded Charleston, 1980, by William and Mae Tudor) that has become a cultural institution. From-scratch biscuits served as sandwiches with named varieties: the "Peppi" (pepperoni and cheese), the "Politician" (bologna, egg, cheese), the "Mary B" and "Ron" (named after loyal customers). Dozens of locations across WV. Musicians have written songs about it. More food institution than dish, but the named biscuit sandwiches and their cultural role qualify.

*Sources: WV Tourism (2022); Register-Herald (2019); Edible Orlando (2024). Four+ sources.*

**Where to eat:** Any of the [Tudor's Biscuit World](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Tudor%27s+Biscuit+World+Charleston+WV) locations across West Virginia. The Charleston area has the highest density. Order the Peppi (pepperoni and cheese) for the local cross-pollination with the Fairmont pepperoni-roll tradition.
