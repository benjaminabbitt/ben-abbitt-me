Duluth and the Iron Range north of it (Hibbing, Virginia, Eveleth, Chisholm, Mountain Iron) make up Minnesota's mining-immigrant food sub-region. Porketta is the most-locked-in dish: an Italian immigrant adaptation of porchetta using American pork cuts and fennel-forward seasoning, propagated through community-event food rather than restaurants.

This list is almost certainly incomplete; Duluth and the Iron Range hold further hyper-local dishes (mining-camp pasties, Slovenian sausages, Cornish saffron buns) that have not yet surfaced in the survey.

> **A note on the Where-to-eat blocks.** Every entry below carries a list of restaurants and, where available, star ratings as of the date this post was published. These are a snapshot. Verify hours and addresses before driving anywhere.

### Porketta — Minnesota Iron Range \{.dish\}

A pork roast heavily seasoned with fennel seed and garlic, slow-roasted, then served sliced or pulled as a sandwich. Brought to Minnesota's Iron Range by Italian immigrants who came to work the mines. Porketta sandwiches are staples at community events, VFW halls, and bars across the Mesabi Range. Not to be confused with Italian porchetta (which uses a different cut and is wrapped in skin); Iron Range porketta is its own distinct adaptation using readily available American pork cuts and fennel-forward seasoning. Essentially unknown outside northeastern Minnesota.

*Sources: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porchetta) (Cuisine of the Midwestern United States); multiple Iron Range food histories.*

**Where to eat:** Iron Ranger, 1085 Grand Ave, St. Paul, MN (4.5 stars, 817 reviews; downstate Twin Cities outpost of the Iron Range food culture). On the Iron Range itself, porketta is VFW and community-event food rather than a restaurant item; check fundraisers in Hibbing, Virginia, Eveleth, and Chisholm. [Sunrise Bakery](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Sunrise+Bakery+Hibbing+MN), Hibbing, sells porketta to-order.
