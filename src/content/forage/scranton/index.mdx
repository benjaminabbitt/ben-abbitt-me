Old Forge in Lackawanna County is a town of roughly 8,000 people with about a dozen pizza shops on or near Main Street. The whole-tray rectangular-pan format with double cheese and sweet chunky tomato sauce is the structural lock; the local vocabulary (a "tray" is a whole, "cuts" not "slices") reinforces the regional identity.

### Old Forge Pizza — Old Forge, PA (near Scranton) \{.dish\}

Rectangular pan pizza with a thick, bready crust (crispy bottom, chewy center), a blend of cheeses often including American cheese, and a sweet, chunky tomato sauce. Originated in the 1920s when Filomena "Grandma" Ghigiarelli baked pizza in large metal pans to feed coal miners. Old Forge (pop ~8,000) has ~12 pizza shops on and around Main Street and claims to be the "pizza capital of the world." Unique terminology: a whole pizza is a "tray," never a "pie"; pieces are "cuts," not "slices." A white version (no sauce, double cheese) is equally popular. The Lackawanna County Pizza Trail connects area shops.

*Sources: The Takeout (2025); La Vida Nomad (2025); Family Vacations US (2026); PA Weekend Fun (2014); Cook Clean Repeat (2025). Six+ sources.*
