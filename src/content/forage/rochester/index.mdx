The Garbage Plate is Rochester's late-night and hangover food, served at Nick Tahou Hots since 1918 and protected by trademark. Competitors copy the format with renamed plates (Rubbish Plate, Dumpster Plate). The dish has never meaningfully escaped Rochester despite decades of food-media coverage.

This list is almost certainly incomplete; Rochester likely holds further hyper-local dishes that have not yet surfaced in the survey.

> **A note on the Where-to-eat blocks.** Every entry below carries a list of restaurants and, where available, star ratings as of the date this post was published. These are a snapshot. Verify hours and addresses before driving anywhere.

### Garbage Plate — Rochester, NY \{.dish\}

A foundation of home fries and/or macaroni salad (or baked beans), topped with your choice of meat (usually two cheeseburger patties or hot dogs), then smothered in a proprietary spicy meat sauce ("hot sauce" in Rochester parlance, not hot pepper sauce), with mustard, onions, and optional ketchup. Created by Alex Tahou at Nick Tahou Hots, established 1918. "Garbage Plate" is a trademarked term. Competitors use names like "Rubbish Plate" or "Dumpster Plate." The definitive late-night/hangover food of Rochester. Has never meaningfully left the city despite decades of food-media coverage.

*Sources: multiple Rochester food guides; [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garbage_plate); Upstate Ramblings (2025). Cross-confirmed.*

**Where to eat:** [Nick Tahou Hots](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Nick+Tahou+Hots+Rochester+NY), 320 W Main St, Rochester (the trademarked original since 1918). Multiple Rochester restaurants serve trademark-renamed versions; ask any local for the current best non-Tahou plate.
