Las Cruces sits closest to Hatch, New Mexico's chile-growing heartland 40 miles north, and anchors the Green Chile Cheeseburger geographically through ingredient supply. The dish itself is statewide; New Mexico runs an official trail.

This list is almost certainly incomplete; Las Cruces and the Mesilla Valley hold further hyper-local dishes that have not yet surfaced in the survey.

### Green Chile Cheeseburger — New Mexico (statewide) \{.dish\}

While green chile itself has gotten national attention, the specific institution of the green chile cheeseburger (a standard burger topped with roasted, peeled Hatch or other NM-grown green chiles and cheese) is so embedded in New Mexico culture that the state runs an official Green Chile Cheeseburger Trail. Every gas station, diner, and restaurant has a version. The annual Hatch Chile Festival draws massive crowds. Outside the Southwest, what you get marketed as "green chile" is usually a pale imitation of the real thing.

*Sources: General knowledge corroborated by Lonely Planet, multiple NM tourism sources.*

**Where to eat:** [Hatch Chile Festival](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Hatch+Chile+Festival+NM), Hatch (Labor Day weekend). [Sparky's](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Sparky%27s+Burgers+Hatch+NM), Hatch (canonical Hatch-anchored green chile cheeseburger). [Buckhorn Tavern](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Buckhorn+Tavern+San+Antonio+NM), San Antonio, NM (Throwdown-with-Bobby-Flay-beating burger). [New Mexico Green Chile Cheeseburger Trail](https://www.newmexico.org/) maps the rest.
