# Snacks

State-by-state inventory of nostalgic regional snacks — chips, candy bars, jerky, and gas-station prepared food that locals grew up with and outsiders have never heard of.

**Archetype:** Old Vienna Red Hot Riplets. Sweet-heat BBQ ridged chip from Fenton, MO. Same recipe since the late 1970s. Banned in California (Prop 65 — the company refuses to reformulate). St. Louis gas stations have them; most other places don't.

**Inclusion test.** A snack qualifies if at least two of these hit:

- Pre-1990 founding, continuous production
- Same recipe, factory, or family across generations
- Locally treated as a generational marker
- Discontinued and revived by descendants
- Used as cultural shorthand for the place itself

produce and full-restaurant items are out.

**Two parallel catalogs.** Live brands go in the main survey. Defunct brands go in a separate archaeology doc — the nostalgia often outlives the product. Pixy Stix, SweeTarts, and Fun Dip were all St. Louis inventions; the products survive, the company doesn't.

## Files

- `regional-gas-station-snacks.md` — main survey, by state. Each entry has founding lineage, current ownership, road-trip findability, and sources.
- `nostalgia-snack-archaeology.md` — defunct and transformed brands.
- `regional-snack-research-tracker.md` — methodology, source hierarchy, what's been excluded and why.
