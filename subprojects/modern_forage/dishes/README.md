# Dishes

Field guide to 190 American dishes that locals eat constantly and outsiders have never heard of.

Examples: a Springfield horseshoe (open-faced sandwich smothered in Welsh-rarebit cheese sauce), a Chicago corn roll tamale (machine-extruded, fitted to a hot dog bun, no relation to Mexican tamales), a South Dakota chislic (deep-fried mutton cubes with saltines).

## Files

- `hyper-local-us-regional-cuisines.md`: the survey. 190 full entries plus 6 notable mentions, 7 regional beverages, 16 regional condiments. Organized by region. Each entry has origin, distribution, sources, and where to eat.
- `regional-cuisine-research-tracker.md`: companion. Records what was investigated, what was excluded and why, and which search mechanisms actually worked.

## Selection criteria

A dish qualifies if it meets at least two of:

- Geographic containment: rarely found more than ~100 miles from origin
- Name-recognition failure: ordering it elsewhere produces a blank stare
- No national chain adoption
- Active local culture, not a museum piece

Nationally famous regional icons (deep-dish pizza, Philly cheesesteaks, Nashville hot chicken, lobster rolls) are out. They've already escaped.

## How to use

Browse by region for trip planning. Search the cross-cutting sections for immigrant-adaptation patterns (Chinese-American, Greek-American, Basque, German-Russian, Cornish mining corridor). Check the tracker when you want to know why something is missing.
