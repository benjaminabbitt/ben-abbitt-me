# Pizza Pot Pie — Chicago Pizza and Oven Grinder Co. (1972)

## What it is

A single-restaurant dish, served at one address in Lincoln Park since 1972. Triple-raised Sicilian dough is draped over a deep ceramic bowl filled with plum-tomato sauce, button mushrooms, mozzarella, and Boston-butt sausage; the bowl bakes upside-down in the oven so that the dough forms a domed lid. At service the bowl is brought to the table on a plate, inverted, and flipped — the menu describes the move as "Dairy Queen blizzard-style" — to reveal the pot pie under its bread dome. Available in half-pound or one-pound portions, with a vegetarian variant.

## Origin

- Attorney **Albert H. Beaver** purchased the property at 2121 N Clark St in April 1972, after a November 1971 fire destroyed the previous building.
- He invested $150,000 in renovations and developed the pizza-pot-pie recipe to anchor the menu. The recipe has not changed since.
- The restaurant has never expanded. There is no second location, no licensing, no franchise, no parent company.

## Why it qualifies (single-restaurant entries in Modern Forage)

Several survey entries are sole-purveyor dishes — Dinglewood Pharmacy's scrambled dog (Columbus, GA, 1918), Louie's Coney Island's bakes (Kokomo, IN, 1960s), Wheel Inn's guberburger (Sedalia, MO, defunct). Pizza pot pie is the most-tenured of the active sole-purveyor entries: 50+ years, same address, same recipe, same flip-the-bowl ritual. The structural argument is unusually clean: the dish *is* the restaurant.

The containment story is also non-trivial. The dish requires:

- Specific ceramic bowls of a specific depth (the dough has to bridge the lip without slumping).
- A triple-raised Sicilian dough recipe whose timing is tuned to the bowl size.
- A baking method that depends on the dough lid forming a seal under the bowl's weight.
- A service ritual (the inversion + flip) that depends on a tableside-trained server and a no-rush dining-room culture.

Each of these is operationally cheap on its own; together they are why no second restaurant has ever copied the form. Pizza pot pie is *not* in the apparatus-locked category like the aquarium smoker; it's in the *operational-routine-locked* category — the dish only works if the entire restaurant is built around delivering it.
