# French Dressing Pizza (Biloxi-Style)

## What it is

Standard pizza, eaten with French (Catalina-style) dressing drizzled or dipped on top of each slice. Not a sauce substitution — the pizza is built and baked in the conventional Italian-American way, with red sauce and mozzarella; the French dressing is added at the table by the eater. The local term is "liquid cheese" for the dressing in this context. Locals call this configuration Biloxi-style pizza or simply "Hugo-style" after the originating shop. The dressing is the defining variable, not the crust or shape.

## Origin

Hugo's (Italian restaurant, 138 Division Street, Biloxi) opened in 1951. Founder Hugo Rungo was a New York City native who stayed on the Gulf Coast after his Keesler Air Force Base service. The French-dressing-on-pizza convention is dated to roughly 1964 in family-history accounts: a high-school student's salad dressing accidentally splashed onto his pizza slice, he tried it, his friends tried it, and it stuck. Hugo's became a hangout for Keesler airmen and the local high-school after-football crowd, and the practice spread along the coast through that customer base. Hilda Ainsworth, longtime Hugo's manager, gave the "liquid cheese" framing and the 1964 date to the *Sun Herald*. Hugo's closed in 2003 but the convention persists across the Gulf Coast.

## Why it's contained

- **Geographic containment**: Garden & Gun frames the practice as having "generations of fans from Pascagoula to Pearlington" — about 90 miles of Mississippi coastline, no further. Doesn't appear in inland Mississippi, Alabama, or Louisiana pizza traditions in any documented form.
- **Name-recognition failure**: Order a Biloxi-style pizza or ask for French dressing on the side anywhere outside the Gulf Coast and you'll get a blank stare or a refusal. The Takeout, PMQ Pizza, and Yahoo Lifestyle all frame the practice as deeply weird to outside readers.
- **No national chain adoption**: No national franchise carries it. The strongest containment-confirming detail: Domino's locations *in the Biloxi market* stock Kraft Catalina packets to meet local demand. No Domino's anywhere else does this. The chain is reacting to the locked geography, not exporting it.
- **Active local culture**: Tom's Extreme Pizzeria (Ocean Springs) makes a house version branded as "Hugo sauce" in homage. Pinterest, food blogs, and family-history blogs from Point Cadet families (Mike and Mary's Kitchen) document continued home and restaurant practice. Multi-vendor: independent Mississippi Gulf Coast pizzerias plus the Biloxi-market Domino's franchises.

**Containment mechanism**: Operational-routine-locked (the dish is the act of pouring French dressing on a pizza slice, an at-table behavior that requires the local customer-base expectation rather than any ingredient or apparatus the rest of the country lacks). Adjacent to Commitment-purchase-locked through the Catalina-on-the-table customer convention.
